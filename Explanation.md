# BackShift — Explanation

**BackShift** is a first-person survival horror game. You're hiding in a
hidden, hot Backrooms-style space under your office, trying to survive 5
minutes until it's safe to sneak back upstairs — while your body dehydrates,
your phone (which proves you're still "on shift") drains, and a boss who
shouldn't even know you're down here keeps getting closer.

## The loop

- Water and phone charge both drain over time. Refill at dispensers/chargers
  (hold E) or drink a carried milk carton for an instant boost.
- Run out of water and you faint (lose). Let your phone die and the boss is
  told exactly where you are — it stops searching and comes straight for you.
- Moving makes noise, and noise is what the boss reacts to from a distance —
  sprinting is loud, walking is quieter, crouching is silent. A one-off
  sound (landing a fall, hitting a wall) fades away in a couple of seconds if
  you go quiet again.
- If it's closing in, duck into a locker to hide. You're invisible and silent
  while hidden, but frozen in place (and your phone/torch get forced off)
  until you come back out.
- Survive the 5-minute timer and you win.

## The boss

Not a simple patrol — it's always loosely aware of you. It walks toward
noise it hears, runs at you if it actually spots you (easier to spot if
you're loud or have your torch on; otherwise it has to be looking right at
you), and gives up and wanders off if you go quiet or hide long enough. It
also gets tired after sustained chasing, giving you a short window to escape
or hide before it's back to full speed.

## Difficulty

Easy/Medium/Hard (picked on the main menu) change how fast the boss starts,
how quickly it ramps up speed over the 5 minutes, how far it can see and
hear, and how easily it gets tired — the level and objective stay the same
either way.
